LingBot-Vision introduces masked boundary modeling for self-supervised vision pretraining

News time: 2026-07-06T18:31:54.905175+00:00 · Source: reddit

LingBot-Vision is a new self-supervised learning framework that rethinks how vision transformers learn geometric structure during pretraining. Rather than masking random image patches — as in MAE or DINO — LingBot-Vision uses a teacher network to dynamically generate categorical boundary fields over each input image, identifying structurally informative edge-like regions that cannot be trivially inferred from surrounding context. These boundary-bearing tokens are then forced into the student’s mask, compelling reconstruction of precisely the non-redundant structural elements critical for depth estimation, segmentation, and scene understanding.

This design addresses two long-standing pitfalls in self-supervised vision: regression drift in continuous boundary targets under exponential moving average (EMA) teachers, and noisy supervision from heuristic edge detectors. By casting boundaries as per-pixel categorical distributions — not raw gradients or distance maps — LingBot-Vision leverages existing centering and sharpening mechanisms from self-distillation to stabilize training. Further, every decoded boundary segment undergoes an a-contrario validation test, a statistical method borrowed from robust detection literature that filters out spurious or statistically insignificant predictions before they supervise the student — ensuring only geometrically meaningful structures contribute to learning.

Empirically, LingBot-Vision achieves a state-of-the-art linear-probe RMSE of 0.296 on NYUv2 depth estimation — narrowly beating DINOv3-7B (0.309) despite using only 161M images, less than one-third of DINOv3’s data budget. Its ViT-L (0.3B) variant matches DINOv3-7B’s performance, suggesting superior data efficiency. Segmentation results on ADE20K and KITTI lag behind DINOv3, while ImageNet classification trails at large scales — indicating a specialization toward geometric reasoning over semantic classification. Crucially, ablation-style encoder initialization experiments show consistent gains across depth completion benchmarks, with performance gaps widening as data volume increases — a strong signal of scalable inductive bias.

The method does not replace but complements existing techniques: like DINOv3, it retains Gram anchoring to prevent feature degradation during long training schedules, suggesting boundary forcing and feature-space regularization are orthogonal improvements. Public checkpoints (four model sizes, Apache-2.0 licensed), open code, and reproducible probe protocols lower barriers to verification — though community skepticism remains about the narrow 0.013 RMSE margin and absence of comparisons against learned masking baselines like ADIOS. As geometric understanding becomes central to embodied AI and robotics, LingBot-Vision offers a principled, teacher-driven path toward more structured, physically grounded representations.
