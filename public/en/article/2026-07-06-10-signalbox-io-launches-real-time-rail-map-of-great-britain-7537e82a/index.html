Signalbox.io Launches Real-Time Rail Map of Great Britain

News time: 2026-07-06T09:38:36+00:00 · Source: hackernews

Signalbox.io has launched a publicly accessible, real-time interactive map of Great Britain’s rail network — a technical achievement that blends legacy railway signalling infrastructure with modern AI-powered smartphone trajectory matching. Unlike conventional transit apps that rely on scheduled timetables or coarse GPS pings, this map visualises actual train positions as they move across the network, updated continuously using live signalling data from the UK’s rail control systems. The platform is designed for both passengers and industry stakeholders, offering granular visibility into train IDs, origin and destination stations, and even intermediate stops and interchanges — all without requiring background location permissions on users’ devices.

What makes Signalbox distinctive is its hybrid data architecture. While most real-time rail maps (e.g., Switzerland’s trafimage.ch or France’s carto.tchoo.net) depend heavily on onboard GPS or Automatic Vehicle Location (AVL) systems, Signalbox leverages the UK’s existing signalling infrastructure — which already records train movements at signal boxes and track circuits — as its primary source. To augment sparse or intermittent signalling updates, the system applies AI-driven map-matching algorithms to anonymised, on-device smartphone sensor snapshots (e.g., accelerometer, gyroscope, barometer, and opportunistic GNSS). These are matched against known train trajectories — not continuous tracking — meaning no persistent location history is collected or stored.

The technology addresses longstanding gaps in UK rail transparency: delays often go unreported until minutes after they occur, and passenger-facing tools rarely reflect actual train positions. By fusing authoritative signalling data with lightweight, privacy-preserving mobile inference, Signalbox delivers higher temporal resolution than timetable-based services while avoiding the battery drain and privacy concerns associated with always-on location tracking. Early community feedback on Hacker News reflects both enthusiasm and healthy scrutiny — with users comparing it to mature European equivalents and questioning how the AI handles degraded GNSS signals or dense urban canyons.

Beyond public utility, Signalbox’s approach hints at a broader shift in transport telemetry: moving away from hardware-dependent AVL toward software-defined, infrastructure-aware sensing. Its ability to work without dedicated hardware or background permissions could serve as a blueprint for other legacy-heavy transport networks seeking real-time awareness without costly retrofits. As rail operators increasingly prioritise predictive maintenance and passenger experience analytics, platforms like Signalbox may become critical intermediaries between physical infrastructure and digital service layers.
