LingBot-Vision introduces masked boundary modeling for self-supervised vision pretraining

News time: 2026-07-07T02:27:41.799166+00:00 · Source: reddit

LingBot-Vision is a new self-supervised learning framework for vision transformers that rethinks how masked modeling should guide representation learning. Rather than randomly masking image patches — a common strategy in methods like MAE or BEiT — LingBot-Vision dynamically identifies structurally informative regions using an online teacher model that predicts dense boundary fields. These boundary-aware tokens are then prioritized for masking, forcing the student to reconstruct precisely those parts of the image that cannot be trivially inferred from surrounding context — such as object edges, depth discontinuities, or surface normals. This design shifts the pretraining objective from generic pixel reconstruction to geometrically grounded, boundary-sensitive recovery.

Two key technical innovations underpin this approach. First, boundary targets are cast as per-pixel categorical distributions — not continuous values — enabling reuse of proven centering and sharpening mechanisms from self-distillation frameworks (e.g., DINO), which prevent representation collapse under exponential moving average (EMA) teachers. Second, decoded segment proposals undergo an a-contrario validation test: a statistical method borrowed from computer vision and remote sensing that filters out spurious or statistically insignificant detections by modeling what would occur by chance alone. Only validated segments are allowed to supervise the student, ensuring signal quality and robustness.

Empirically, LingBot-Vision achieves a state-of-the-art linear-probe RMSE of 0.296 on NYUv2 depth estimation — narrowly beating DINOv3-7B (0.309) despite using only 161M images, less than one-third of DINOv3’s training data. Its ViT-L (0.3B) variant matches DINOv3-7B’s performance, suggesting strong data efficiency. Segmentation results on ADE20K and KITTI trail DINOv3, and ImageNet classification lags at larger scales — though smaller models (ViT-B/S) lead their class on linear probing. Crucially, encoder initialization experiments show consistent gains across depth completion benchmarks when swapping in LingBot-Vision features, with performance gaps widening as data volume increases.

The method does not replace existing stabilization techniques: like DINOv3, it retains Gram anchoring to prevent feature degradation during long training schedules — indicating boundary forcing complements, rather than supplants, established regularization strategies. While the reported metrics are compelling, the community cautions that the 0.013 RMSE advantage over DINOv3 falls within typical probe sensitivity ranges, and ablations against learned masking baselines (e.g., ADIOS) remain unreported. Public checkpoints on Hugging Face invite independent verification — a necessary next step given prior concerns around reproducibility in related work.
