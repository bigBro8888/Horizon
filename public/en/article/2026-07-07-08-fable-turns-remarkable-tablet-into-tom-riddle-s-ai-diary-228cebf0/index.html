Fable Turns reMarkable Tablet into Tom Riddle’s AI Diary

News time: 2026-07-07T02:27:42.252712+00:00 · Source: hackernews

A clever new open-source project named Fable transforms the reMarkable e-ink tablet — beloved by writers and note-takers for its paper-like feel — into an immersive, Harry Potter–themed interactive diary modeled after Tom Riddle’s enchanted journal. Built on the reMarkable Paper Pro hardware, Fable leverages local large language models (LLMs) to enable handwritten input that fades away like magical ink, followed by AI-generated responses rendered in elegant, script-like handwriting. The experience deliberately evokes the eerie charm of the fictional diary: users write questions or thoughts by hand, watch their ink vanish, and receive replies as if conversing with a sentient, slightly ominous presence.

Unlike cloud-based chatbots, Fable runs entirely offline on the device — a technical feat given the reMarkable’s modest hardware (ARM Cortex-A9 CPU, 512 MB RAM). It integrates lightweight LLMs via frameworks like Ollama and custom fine-tuned models optimized for roleplay, ensuring privacy and low latency. The UI mimics the aged parchment aesthetic of Riddle’s diary, complete with subtle animations and typographic flourishes that reinforce the fantasy theme without compromising usability.

The project is not just a novelty — it exemplifies a growing trend in creative computing: repurposing minimalist hardware with generative AI to craft emotionally resonant, context-aware interfaces. By grounding AI interaction in physical gesture (handwriting) and narrative framing (a beloved literary artifact), Fable sidesteps the sterile ‘chat window’ paradigm and invites playful, embodied engagement. Yet this very strength sparks debate: several commenters on Hacker News raised ethical concerns about romanticizing a fictional object tied to manipulation, coercion, and psychological harm — drawing uncomfortable parallels to real-world risks of persuasive AI.

Still, developers praise Fable’s technical execution and accessibility. Its GitHub repository includes clear setup instructions, modular architecture, and compatibility with other local LLM toolchains like remarkable-lm. As edge AI matures and devices like reMarkable become viable LLM hosts, projects like Fable point toward a future where AI isn’t just functional — it’s expressive, thematic, and deeply personal.
