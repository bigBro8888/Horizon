MIRA: 5B-parameter multiplayer world model for Rocket League runs at 20 fps on one B200

News time: 2026-07-08T04:29:38.096434+00:00 · Source: reddit

MIRA — short for Multiplayer Interactive World Models — is a groundbreaking step toward real-time, multi-agent simulation in complex 3D environments. Developed jointly by General Intuition, Kyutai, and Epic Games, MIRA is not just another video prediction model: it’s a fully interactive, closed-loop world model capable of simulating four-player Rocket League matches end-to-end, where all agents (including human players) act within the same learned latent dynamics. Trained exclusively on 10,000 hours of synthetic gameplay data — generated by AI-controlled agents rather than human recordings — MIRA sidesteps the scarcity and noise of real-world behavioral data while ensuring perfect state-action alignment across all players.

What makes MIRA especially notable is its deployment efficiency. With 5 billion parameters, it achieves real-time inference at 20 frames per second for full 2v2 matches on a single NVIDIA B200 GPU — a feat previously unattainable for models of this scale and interactivity. This performance leverages Blackwell architecture advantages, including high-bandwidth memory and optimized FP8 inference, enabling low-latency, frame-by-frame generation conditioned on all four players’ simultaneous actions. Unlike autoregressive language models or offline simulators, MIRA operates as a latent diffusion world model: it synthesizes future game states in compressed latent space, then decodes them into visual frames — effectively running an entire physics-aware, decision-driven virtual arena inside the model.

The release is unusually comprehensive: an open online demo lets anyone experience live interaction; a technical report details the representation autoencoder architecture, training pipeline, and synthetic data curation strategy; the GitHub repository includes training scripts, inference tools, and model weights; and a curated 1,000-hour subset of the 10k-hour dataset is publicly available for reproducibility and extension. At ICML 2024, attendees can even play with physical PlayStation controllers at Booth 111 — underscoring MIRA’s design goal: bridging research-grade modeling with tangible, embodied interaction.

MIRA sits at the intersection of several fast-moving frontiers: world modeling, multi-agent reinforcement learning, synthetic data generation, and real-time AI systems. Its success challenges assumptions about data requirements — proving that carefully engineered synthetic data can outperform noisy human demonstrations in structured, rule-bound domains. More broadly, it signals a shift from passive observation to active co-simulation: where AI doesn’t just predict or imitate, but hosts and sustains shared, responsive virtual worlds.
