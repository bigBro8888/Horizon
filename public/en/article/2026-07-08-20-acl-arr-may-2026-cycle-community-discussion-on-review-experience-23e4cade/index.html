ACL ARR May 2026 Cycle: Community Discussion on Review Experience

News time: 2026-07-08T04:29:38.096396+00:00 · Source: reddit

A new Reddit thread titled 'ACL ARR May 2026[D]' invites researchers across the NLP and computational linguistics community to share firsthand experiences from the most recent ACL Advanced Reviewing Repository (ARR) cycle — specifically the May 2026 submission and review period. The post, created by user /u/Historical_Pause247, explicitly solicits perspectives from three key stakeholder groups: reviewers who evaluated papers, authors who submitted work, and Area Chairs (ACs) who managed review assignments and meta-reviews. It asks open-ended questions about workflow challenges, fairness perceptions, timeline adherence, and — notably — average scores within AC-managed paper lots, suggesting interest in consistency and calibration across review panels.

This discussion comes at a pivotal moment for ARR. After operating eight-week cycles from October 2022 through February 2025, the program transitioned to ten-week cycles starting with the May 2025 cycle — a change driven by community feedback seeking more time for thorough reviewing. The May 2026 cycle is thus the second under this revised schedule, making it an important data point for assessing whether the longer timeline has improved review quality or reduced reviewer burden.

According to an official explanatory letter published on the ARR website, the May 2026 cycle saw unprecedented submission volume: 17,087 papers — significantly higher than prior cycles — while relying on only 1,424 qualified Area Chairs and 10,636 reviewers. To maintain review capacity, the ARR leadership made the difficult decision to increase reciprocal service requirements for submitting authors — meaning more authors were asked to serve as reviewers. This policy shift reflects systemic pressure on the volunteer-based peer review infrastructure supporting top-tier NLP venues.

For authors targeting ACL 2026, the May 2026 ARR cycle remains relevant: papers reviewed in earlier cycles (e.g., October 2025 or January 2026) can be committed to the main conference, but those entering via May 2026 will feed into subsequent commitment windows. Ultimately, this Reddit thread serves not just as anecdotal venting, but as grassroots sense-making — helping researchers collectively interpret evolving norms, workload trade-offs, and equity concerns in one of NLP’s most influential review platforms.
