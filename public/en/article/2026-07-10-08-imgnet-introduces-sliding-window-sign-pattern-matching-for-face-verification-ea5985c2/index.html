IMGNet Introduces Sliding-Window Sign Pattern Matching for Face Verification

News time: 2026-07-10T05:05:39.994279+00:00 · Source: reddit

A solo researcher from Indonesia has introduced IMGNet — a novel face verification architecture that abandons the conventional cosine similarity metric in favor of a biologically and linguistically inspired approach: sliding-window sign pattern matching over embedding vectors. Rather than measuring the global angular alignment between two face embeddings (as cosine similarity does), IMGNet compares local sign consistency — whether corresponding dimensions across overlapping windows of two embeddings share the same positive/negative polarity. This idea draws inspiration from Javanese and Sundanese languages, where distinct surface forms ('matur suwun' vs. 'hatur nuhun') encode identical semantic meaning — suggesting identity resides not in absolute values but in relational structure.

At its core, IMGNet introduces the SW Block, a custom neural layer that replaces standard convolutions with multi-scale relational operations. For each dimension in an embedding, it computes pairwise differences to neighbors within prime-sized windows (3×3, 5×5, 7×7), yielding 240 difference signals per position; these are then processed by a small MLP to produce output channels. Crucially, training uses the newly proposed IMG Sign MSE Loss — a loss function defined exclusively on sign agreement, with no dependence on embedding amplitude. Empirical results show this loss is significantly more stable during training (±0.40% accuracy variance) compared to amplitude-sensitive variants (±2.25%).

IMGNet achieves 96.27% accuracy on the Labeled Faces in the Wild (LFW) benchmark when trained end-to-end on CASIA-WebFace — competitive with many lightweight models despite its modest 10.58 MB size. More remarkably, when applied post-hoc to precomputed ArcFace (buffalo_l) embeddings — without any fine-tuning — IMGNet’s sign-based scoring attains 99.58% on LFW, just 0.24% below ArcFace+cosine. This suggests sign pattern consistency is an emergent, robust property of high-quality face embeddings, independent of how they were trained. The system also integrates a three-metric voting scheme (IMG Sign Score, AMP IMG Score, Chain Score), all sharing a single threshold and yielding intuitive match/uncertain/different decisions.

An unexpected observation further hints at deeper structure: when the same facial region is occluded across images of the same person, the resulting embedding deltas spike at similar dimensional indices — whereas occlusions on different people trigger spikes in distinct locations. This implies the sliding-window loss may implicitly organize embedding dimensions along anatomically or semantically meaningful axes — a hypothesis still awaiting formal validation. Ultimately, IMGNet challenges a long-standing default in deep face recognition: that cosine similarity is the only reasonable choice. Instead, it advocates for co-designing metrics and losses — a principle the author calls 'metric-loss alignment'.
