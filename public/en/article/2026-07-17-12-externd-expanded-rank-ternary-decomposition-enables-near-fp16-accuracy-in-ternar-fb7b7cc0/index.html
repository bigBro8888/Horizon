ExTernD: Expanded-Rank Ternary Decomposition Enables Near-FP16 Accuracy in Ternary LLM Quantization

News time: 2026-07-17T04:21:51.797809+00:00 · Source: reddit

A new post-training quantization (PTQ) method called ExTernD challenges long-standing limitations in ternary neural network deployment for large language models. Unlike conventional ternary quantization—which forces weight matrices into fixed-rank ternary forms and suffers from an inherent accuracy ceiling—ExTernD introduces an expanded-rank ternary decomposition. The core innovation lies in representing each original weight matrix as the product of two ternary matrices (with entries in {−1, 0, +1}) and a scalable diagonal scaling matrix. Crucially, the inner rank of this decomposition is no longer fixed; it can be increased arbitrarily to recover fine-grained weight information, thereby closing the gap with full-precision (FP16) inference.

This design sidesteps the fundamental trade-off between hardware efficiency and model fidelity that has plagued prior ternary approaches. While binary or 4-bit quantization often demands complex calibration or fine-tuning, ExTernD achieves high accuracy without retraining, making it a true post-training method. Empirical results suggest that even modest increases in rank—requiring only slightly more VRAM than standard 4-bit quantization—yield dramatic accuracy improvements, especially on downstream language tasks like perplexity and zero-shot evaluation.

What makes ExTernD particularly compelling is its synergy with emerging hardware. Ternary arithmetic enables ultra-efficient compute using simple integer operations and low-power logic gates—ideal for edge AI accelerators and neuromorphic chips. By preserving ternary sparsity and simplicity while lifting the rank bottleneck, ExTernD unlocks practical high-accuracy ternary LLMs for real-world deployment. It also avoids the overhead of adapter-based methods like LoRA or QLoRA, which introduce additional parameters and runtime latency.

The technique sits at the intersection of matrix approximation theory and hardware-aware AI optimization. Its expanded-rank formulation draws inspiration from low-rank decomposition methods used in model compression—but unlike ASVD or LoRA, it strictly maintains ternary constraints across all components. This ensures compatibility with ternary-native hardware while offering tunable accuracy via rank selection. As industry pushes toward energy-efficient AI, ExTernD demonstrates that ternary isn’t just a compromise—it’s a scalable foundation for next-generation inference.
