Inaugural RTCA Workshop Announced for NeurIPS 2026

News time: 2026-07-17T04:21:51.798001+00:00 · Source: reddit

The machine learning community has a new focal point for one of AI’s most demanding frontiers: real-time, multimodal conversational agents. The inaugural Real-Time Conversational Agents (RTCA) workshop—set to take place on December 11 or 12, 2026 in Sydney, Australia, as part of the NeurIPS conference—has officially opened its call for papers and demos. Unlike traditional conversational AI research centered on offline text generation or batch-mode speech processing, RTCA explicitly targets systems that operate in the moment: listening, watching, thinking, and speaking simultaneously, with human-level responsiveness and natural turn-taking.

This shift reflects a broader industry pivot—from chatbots confined to screens to embodied agents inhabiting shared physical or digital spaces. Think voice assistants that interrupt politely, avatars that maintain eye contact while you speak, or co-pilots that react mid-sentence to your gestures and tone. Achieving such behavior demands solving problems long ignored in static paradigms: hard latency budgets (often under 200 ms), full-duplex audio processing, streaming cross-modal alignment between speech, vision, and language, and robust handling of backchannels, overlaps, and interruptions.

The workshop organizers—researchers from Tavus, King’s College London, University of Verona, and Queen Mary University of London—frame RTCA around three core challenges: real-time generation under strict latency constraints; interactional naturalness (e.g., prosody, gaze, floor management); and rigorous evaluation of live systems, not post-hoc transcripts. Topics span streaming ASR and TTS, incremental LLM decoding, real-time talking-head synthesis, multimodal memory grounding, and even ethical concerns like consent and deepfake mitigation in live interactions.

Submissions fall into three categories: full papers (up to 8 pages), short papers (up to 4 pages), and demo papers (up to 2 pages), all double-blind reviewed using the official NeurIPS 2026 style. Crucially, the workshop is non-archival—authors retain full publishing rights. With submissions due August 29, 2026, and notifications by September 29, RTCA arrives at a pivotal moment: just as full-duplex models (e.g., DuplexIO, PersonaPlex) and streaming multimodal frameworks mature, the field urgently needs shared benchmarks, evaluation protocols, and a unified vocabulary—exactly what this workshop aims to catalyze.
