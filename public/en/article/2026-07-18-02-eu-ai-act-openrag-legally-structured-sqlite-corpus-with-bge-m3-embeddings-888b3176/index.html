EU AI Act OpenRAG: Legally Structured SQLite Corpus with BGE-M3 Embeddings

News time: 2026-07-18T04:12:28.431751+00:00 · Source: reddit

A groundbreaking open resource for legal AI has emerged: EU AI Act OpenRAG — a meticulously structured, publicly available SQLite database built from Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, the landmark EU Artificial Intelligence Act. Unlike conventional RAG datasets that rely on arbitrary sliding-window text segmentation, this corpus respects the law’s formal hierarchy: each chunk corresponds to a legally meaningful unit — an article paragraph, a recital, a definition from Article 3, or a discrete point in an annex. This results in 933 semantically coherent, human-annotated chunks, each enriched with a normalized 1024-dimensional BGE-M3 embedding — a state-of-the-art multilingual embedding model supporting dense, sparse, and multi-vector retrieval.

The dataset goes beyond raw text: it embeds precise metadata including official EUR-Lex hyperlinks, Article 113 application-date information, and narrowly defined labels that separate direct textual classification (e.g., 'Article 5 violation') from broader regulatory regime associations (e.g., 'high-risk AI system'). Ambiguous cases are explicitly marked NULL rather than forced into categories — a design choice prioritizing legal fidelity over algorithmic convenience. All derivations, label audits, licensing terms (CC BY-SA 4.0), and methodological limitations are transparently documented, not just the favorable metrics.

Evaluation against the AI Act Evaluation Benchmark reveals tangible improvements: structural chunking boosts scenario-based article recall@20 from 0.449 to 0.541 and QA article hit@10 from 0.898 to 0.927. Interestingly, overall RAG classification performance remains nearly identical — suggesting that downstream generative behavior, not retrieval granularity alone, dominates classification tasks. This nuanced insight underscores the importance of task-aware evaluation in legal AI.

Published on Hugging Face and accompanied by full reproducibility artifacts, EU AI Act OpenRAG fills a critical gap at the intersection of regulatory compliance and technical rigor. It enables researchers to benchmark retrieval fidelity, test legal reasoning pipelines, and build auditable compliance assistants — all grounded in the actual structure of the law, not approximations. For practitioners building RAG systems for legal tech, this isn’t just another dataset; it’s a new baseline for what ‘legally aware’ AI should mean.
