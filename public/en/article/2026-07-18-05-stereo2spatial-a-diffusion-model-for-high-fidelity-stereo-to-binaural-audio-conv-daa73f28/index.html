Stereo2Spatial: A Diffusion Model for High-Fidelity Stereo-to-Binaural Audio Conversion

News time: 2026-07-18T04:12:28.431512+00:00 · Source: reddit

Stereo2Spatial is a newly released open-source AI model that transforms standard stereo music tracks into immersive, spatialized binaural audio—enabling headphone listeners to experience music with realistic 3D sound localization. Developed over six months by independent researcher Francis La Bounty, the system addresses a persistent gap in the audio ecosystem: while spatial audio formats like Dolby Atmos and Apple Spatial Audio are gaining traction, the vast majority of existing music libraries remain locked in stereo. Rather than relying on manual re-mixing or legacy tools, Stereo2Spatial uses modern generative AI techniques to automate this conversion with unprecedented fidelity.

The model evolved through two major architectural iterations. The first version operated in the latent space of EAR-VAE—a perceptually optimized variational autoencoder trained on high-fidelity music—but struggled with quality due to distribution mismatch: EAR-VAE was designed for stereo encoding/decoding, not for reconstructing individual channels of complex multi-channel outputs like 7.1.4. This led to artifacts and instability during long-context generation. To overcome this, the developer pivoted to raw waveform modeling—a more demanding but ultimately more accurate approach.

Training the waveform model proved challenging: early attempts suffered from catastrophic instability after ~60K–80K steps, despite tuning learning rates, gradient clipping, and scaling strategies. The breakthrough came from adopting amplitude lifting, inspired by the WavFlow paper: input waveforms are clipped to ±4.0 (not ±1.0 as in WavFlow) and scaled so their RMS equals 0.33, then multiplied by 3—effectively mapping audio into a [-12, 12] range. This simple yet critical preprocessing step stabilized training completely. The final model was trained for 20 days across two stages on two NVIDIA A6000 GPUs, using over 7,600 diverse music tracks.

Stereo2Spatial ships with optional mix-style conditioning for controllable output characteristics and includes a polished Windows desktop application for seamless inference—no command-line expertise required. Both the binaural waveform model (v2) and the earlier latent-space version (v1) are released under the Apache 2.0 license, alongside full training/inference code and detailed case studies. With its focus on practical usability, technical transparency, and audio-perceptual rigor, Stereo2Spatial sets a new benchmark for accessible, high-quality spatial audio upmixing.
