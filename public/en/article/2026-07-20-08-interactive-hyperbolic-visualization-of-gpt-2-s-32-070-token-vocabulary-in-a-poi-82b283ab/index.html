What 32,070 GPT-2 Tokens Look Like in Hyperbolic Space

News time: 2026-07-20T04:49:42.055364+00:00 · Source: reddit

A striking new interactive visualization transforms GPT-2’s entire vocabulary—32,070 tokens from the GPT-2-small model—into a navigable hyperbolic space. Instead of flattening embeddings onto a Euclidean plane, the creator embeds them directly into a 2D Poincaré ball, a canonical model of hyperbolic geometry where distances and angles behave fundamentally differently than in flat space. The result is not a trained or optimized layout, but an exact geometric arrangement that reveals the vocabulary’s intrinsic hierarchical structure: one dominant semantic tree (~2,300 tokens), dozens of smaller family trees, and roughly 6,700 isolated tokens with no close neighbors.

What makes this especially compelling is its accessibility and interactivity. The web application runs smoothly on mobile devices—no GPU or heavy dependencies required. Users can drag to rotate the view, pinch to zoom, and tap any token to instantly recenter the entire Poincaré ball around it. This recentering uses Möbius transformations, the natural isometries of hyperbolic space that preserve angles and hyperbolic distances—equivalent to ‘moving the camera’ without distorting the underlying geometry. Tapping neighboring tokens allows seamless exploration of semantic neighborhoods, turning abstract embeddings into an intuitive, almost spatial experience.

The choice of hyperbolic geometry is not arbitrary. Trees—and hierarchies more broadly—embed poorly in Euclidean space without distortion: as depth increases, sibling nodes must be squeezed into exponentially shrinking angular regions. In contrast, hyperbolic space offers exponentially growing ‘room’ near the boundary of the Poincaré ball, making it ideal for representing branching, tree-like relationships. This principle underlies Poincaré embeddings, first proposed in 2017 for modeling taxonomies and knowledge graphs—and now demonstrated concretely at scale using real, unmodified GPT-2 token embeddings.

Unlike many interpretability tools that rely on dimensionality reduction (e.g., t-SNE or UMAP) or post-hoc probing, this visualization requires zero training, fine-tuning, or optimization. It uses only the raw, pre-trained token embeddings—vectors learned during language modeling—and places them directly into hyperbolic space using closed-form geometric mapping. That fidelity, combined with real-time navigation and pedagogical clarity, positions it as both a research artifact and a teaching tool—bridging geometric deep learning, NLP semantics, and human-centered model interpretation.
